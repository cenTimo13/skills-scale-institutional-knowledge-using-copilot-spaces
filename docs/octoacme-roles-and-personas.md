# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove blockers, foster team collaboration, and ensure sprint commitments are achievable. They champion agile practices and create an environment where teams can deliver effectively.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, retrospectives, and sprint reviews
- Remove impediments and blockers that prevent team progress
- Coach the team on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Protect the team from external distractions and scope creep
- Track sprint metrics and team velocity
- Foster psychological safety and team collaboration

### Goals
- Ensure predictable and sustainable delivery velocity
- Maximize team productivity and morale
- Reduce organizational friction and blockers
- Build self-organizing, high-performing teams

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation of standup meetings
- Sprint planning and retrospective sessions
- One-on-one coaching with team members
- Escalation discussions with Project Managers for organizational blockers

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Coaches on agile practices, removes blockers, facilitates team ceremonies
- **Project Managers**: Partners on removing organizational impediments and aligning on timelines
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on backlog refinement and sprint goal setting

---

## Technical Lead

### Role Summary
Technical Leads set the technical direction, oversee code quality, mentor developers, and drive architectural decisions. They ensure technical excellence while balancing delivery needs with long-term maintainability.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical architecture and design patterns
- Review and approve critical design decisions
- Mentor developers and conduct code reviews
- Resolve complex technical challenges and production issues
- Establish coding standards and best practices
- Evaluate and recommend tools, frameworks, and technologies
- Represent engineering in cross-functional planning

### Goals
- Maintain high code quality and architectural integrity
- Build scalable, maintainable systems
- Grow technical capabilities across the team
- Balance technical debt with feature delivery

### Typical Communication
- Technical design documents and architecture decision records (ADRs)
- Code review feedback and pair programming sessions
- Technical planning sessions with Product and Project Managers
- Mentoring conversations with developers

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Mentors, reviews code, pairs on complex problems, provides technical guidance
- **Project Managers**: Advises on technical risks, estimates, and dependencies
- **Product Managers**: Translates product requirements into technical solutions, advises on feasibility
- **QA Lead**: Collaborates on test strategies and quality standards

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers research user needs, design intuitive user flows and interfaces, and ensure accessibility and design consistency. They advocate for the user experience throughout the product development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Design user flows and information architecture
- Ensure accessibility standards (WCAG) are met
- Maintain design systems and component libraries
- Collaborate with developers on implementation feasibility
- Validate designs through user feedback and analytics

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Increase user satisfaction and task completion rates
- Maintain design consistency across products
- Reduce development rework through early validation

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- Usability test findings and user research reports
- Design specifications and handoff documentation
- Collaboration sessions with Product Managers and Developers

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on feature definition, user research, and success metrics
- **Developers**: Partners on implementation feasibility, provides design specs and assets
- **QA Lead**: Coordinates on usability testing and acceptance criteria validation
- **Stakeholders**: Presents design concepts and incorporates feedback

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads develop and maintain comprehensive test strategies, coordinate quality assurance activities, and ensure defect tracking and reporting processes are effective. They champion quality throughout the development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategy and quality standards
- Coordinate manual and automated testing efforts
- Manage defect lifecycle and regression testing
- Establish test environments and data management practices
- Lead test planning and risk-based testing prioritization
- Mentor team members on testing practices
- Report on quality metrics and test coverage

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases with minimal defects
- Reduce time spent on bug fixes and rework
- Increase test automation coverage
- Shift quality left in the development process

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and quality reports
- Bug triage meetings and defect status updates
- Test case reviews and automation strategy discussions
- Quality gate checkpoints during release planning

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates on testability, reviews test coverage, reports and triages defects
- **Project Managers**: Reports on quality metrics, identifies quality risks, advises on release readiness
- **Product Managers**: Validates acceptance criteria, provides quality insights on features
- **Technical Lead**: Aligns on test automation architecture and quality standards
- **UX Designer**: Coordinates on usability testing and user acceptance validation

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts translate business needs into clear requirements, analyze potential solutions, and bridge the communication gap between business stakeholders and technical teams. They ensure solutions align with business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements
- Analyze business processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Create functional specifications and user stories
- Facilitate requirements workshops with stakeholders
- Validate solutions meet business needs
- Perform gap analysis and feasibility studies
- Support user acceptance testing (UAT) planning

### Goals
- Ensure solutions deliver measurable business value
- Reduce scope ambiguity and requirement changes
- Improve communication between business and technical teams
- Identify opportunities for process optimization

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and functional specifications
- Stakeholder interviews and workshop facilitation
- Business case presentations and ROI analysis
- User story refinement sessions

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on roadmap prioritization, translates business requirements into product features
- **Project Managers**: Partners on scope definition, requirement tracking, and stakeholder management
- **Developers**: Clarifies requirements, provides business context, validates technical approaches
- **Stakeholder Champion**: Works closely to gather stakeholder feedback and validate solutions
- **QA Lead**: Defines acceptance criteria and supports UAT planning

---

## Stakeholder Champion

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Champions serve as the main point of contact for stakeholder groups, organize feedback loops, and ensure stakeholder perspectives are represented in project decisions. They facilitate communication between project teams and the broader organization.

### Responsibilities
- Represent stakeholder groups in project discussions
- Organize and facilitate stakeholder feedback sessions
- Communicate project updates and gather input
- Identify and escalate stakeholder concerns
- Coordinate change management and adoption activities
- Ensure stakeholder requirements are captured and addressed
- Support training and communication rollout

### Goals
- Maximize stakeholder satisfaction and adoption
- Reduce change resistance through early engagement
- Ensure stakeholder voices are heard in decision-making
- Facilitate smooth organizational transitions

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder updates and newsletters
- Feedback sessions and town halls
- Change management communications
- Executive briefings and status reports

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Partners on stakeholder communication plans and change management
- **Product Managers**: Gathers stakeholder feedback on roadmap and feature priorities
- **Business Analyst**: Collaborates on requirements gathering and validation
- **Developers**: Shares stakeholder feedback on usability and feature requests
- **All roles**: Acts as primary liaison for stakeholder questions and feedback

## Role Interaction Summary

The OctoAcme team structure emphasizes collaboration across roles:

- **Core Delivery Team**: Developers, Technical Lead, Scrum Master, QA Lead, UX Designer
  - Daily collaboration on feature development and quality
  - Participates in sprint planning, standups, and demos
  
- **Product & Strategy**: Product Manager (PdM), Business Analyst
  - Define what to build and why
  - Prioritize backlog and validate business value
  
- **Coordination & Communication**: Project Manager (PM), Stakeholder Champion
  - Coordinate delivery, manage risks, and facilitate stakeholder engagement
  - Maintain communication plans and status reporting
  
- **Cross-functional Ceremonies**:
  - Sprint planning: Core Delivery Team + PM + PdM
  - Stakeholder reviews: PM + PdM + Stakeholder Champion + Technical Lead
  - Technical design reviews: Technical Lead + Developers + QA Lead + UX Designer
  - Retrospectives: Core Delivery Team + PM + PdM (facilitated by Scrum Master)

For detailed handoff and escalation paths, see [Risk Management & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md).
